Selecting Your Classes

On this page, you can learn more about how to prepare for selecting your classes.

Basic Info Choosing Courses (You are here)

The classes that you choose are 100% up to you as long as you can fit them into your schedule. Use Lane's Course Catalog in order to view them all.

In general, there are four main things that you should be thinking about.

Challenge

Are you challenging yourself with these classes or are you overwhelming yourself?

Graduation Requirements

Looking at how your classes fit into a schedule for the long run, do they all meet your graduation requirements?

Future Interests

(if you know what you want to do) Do these classes tell a story of your progression towards a goal/career?

Prerequisites

Do you meet the prerequisites for this class?

To challenge yourself would be to know your boundaries and work smartly with them. Students usually stress themselves out with AP classes because they think that's all colleges are looking at, but this is a misconception. Your classes shouldn't be easy, but at the end of the day they should tell a story of where you are going in life. If you're going to major in Mechanical Engineering, I really doubt that colleges will care about whether you took AP European History or not.
When you are looking at courses on Lane's Course Selections page, make sure that its level of difficulty, time of commitment/homework, and career aspiriations will work for you.

Like the general info page previously explained, as a student you have both Lane's basic highschool graduation requirements as well as that of your concentration (Core, CS, etc). Keep class credits in mind when choosing your classes.

Some classes will require something before you are allowed to take them, like a certain GPA or having taken a related previous course. When you view classes on Lane's Course Selections page, you can see what these prerequisites are.
